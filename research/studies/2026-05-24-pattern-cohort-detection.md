# Pattern cohort detector-confirmation — first study (2026-05-24)

## Question

When V1 sensitivity analysis identifies a cfg threshold variable as binding at
the watch→A+ boundary (variables that flip N candidates `watch → aplus` when
relaxed), do the Phase 13 chart-shape detectors (`vcp`, `flat_base`,
`cup_with_handle`, `high_tight_flag`, `double_bottom_w`) confirm the chart
shape on those promoted candidates?

The architectural motivation is gotcha #27: production
`swing.pipeline.runner._step_pattern_detect` gates on `bucket == 'aplus'` BY
DESIGN — so V1's persisted record carries NO detector verdict for any
candidate that was `watch` at production time. We cannot answer "is the
relaxation buying real chart-shape signal or just classification volume?"
without invoking the detectors on the loosened-A+ cohort separately.

## Null hypothesis

H0: detector-pass rate on the promoted cohort is statistically indistinguishable
from a random sample of `bucket='aplus'` candidates over the same evaluation
runs. I.e., the threshold relaxation buys NO additional chart-shape signal —
the detectors confirm at the same rate they would on any aplus cohort. Under
H0, the V2 OHLCV backtest verdict (NEGATIVE for `vcp.tightness_range_factor=1.005`
at merge `e0a9edd`) is fully explained: the promoted candidates have indistinct
chart-shape signal AND the breakout-trigger gate is the binding mechanism.

## Baseline

The reference cohort is the **+67 watch→aplus flips at
`vcp.tightness_range_factor=1.005`** identified by the V2 OHLCV criterion-evaluator
full-63-eval-run reproduction at smoke artifact
`exports/diagnostics/aplus-sensitivity-v2-20260524T205849Z.md`. 15 unique tickers
span eval_runs 6-43. This was the top binding variable by `max_delta_aplus`.

Backtest for the same cohort was already shipped at merge `e0a9edd` (17 patterns
/ 5 triggered / 0 closed / -0.18R mean unrealized). The backtest verdict
NEGATIVE for the top binding variable motivated the operator-paired pivot
hypothesis that "+75 classification flips DOES NOT equal +75 actionable trades"
— breakout-trigger conversion is a separate gating mechanism upstream of
pattern verification. This study extends the diagnostic ONE LAYER DEEPER: even
PRE-breakout-trigger, did the detectors confirm the chart shape?

## Methodology

- Cohort substrate: `exports/research/cohorts/tightness_1.005_flips_67.csv`
  (67 rows; 15 unique tickers; operator-supplied per spec §L.4 derivation from
  V2 OHLCV smoke drill-down rows where `sweep_point=1.005` AND `to_bucket=aplus`).
- Harness: `python -m swing.cli diagnose pattern-cohort-detect
  --cohort-csv ... --db ~/swing-data/swing.db --window-mode per-window
  --template-match on`.
- Reads Shape A OHLCV via re-export of V2 OHLCV evaluator's reader (OQ-3 LOCK).
- Invokes all 5 production detectors (OQ-1 LOCK) for each candidate-window
  generated by `generate_candidate_windows(bars, "zigzag_pivot", ...)` (OQ-4
  production-parity).
- Template-match Pass 2 ON (OQ-6 production-parity); composite_score per
  `swing.patterns.composite.compute_composite_score`.
- Window mode `per-window` (NON-production-default; analytical bias per OQ-7) —
  emit one row per `(cohort_entry, pattern_class, window)` rather than only the
  last window.

## Interpretation

**Pending Results.** Three a priori interpretation scenarios:

- **S1 (orthogonal signal):** detector-pass cohort has differentiated
  breakout-trigger rate vs detector-fail cohort. The threshold relaxation buys
  real chart-shape candidates and the negative backtest is driven by
  unrelated factors (small sample / market regime / breakout-trigger threshold
  calibration). Implication: operator-paired investigation of the
  trigger-conversion stage warranted.
- **S2 (no orthogonal signal):** detector-pass and detector-fail cohorts have
  indistinguishable trigger rates. H0 confirmed; the threshold relaxation
  buys classification noise (small `pattern.geometric_score` variation that
  crosses the cfg threshold but does NOT shift detector verdict). Implication:
  shadow-promote the V2 OHLCV finding "all 5 binding variables NEGATIVE on
  detector-confirmation grounds" + DO NOT proceed with any cfg threshold
  relaxation deployment.
- **S3 (detector-fail dominant):** the promoted cohort has very few detector
  passes regardless of class. Stronger version of S2: the threshold variable
  is selecting candidates that lie OUTSIDE the detectors' acceptance regions
  entirely. Implication: same as S2.

## Limitations

- **L1 — pattern_exemplars corpus drift.** Per method-record §K.4 L1.
- **L2 — OHLCV archive bar-content TEMPORAL mutation.** Per cumulative gotcha
  #26 + method-record §K.4 L2.
- **L3 — current_stage uses current DB state.** Per method-record §K.4 L3.
- **L4 — Parallel-archive freshness desync.** Per cumulative gotcha #24 +
  method-record §K.4 L4.
- **L5 — Backtest cross-tabulation requires N=17 → 67 mapping completeness.**
  The backtest at `e0a9edd` evaluated 17 patterns from the same cohort (a
  subset; some entries did not breakout-trigger). Cross-tabulation completeness
  depends on the trigger-detection criterion's coverage of the 67-entry cohort.
- **L6 — Single-variable single-cohort.** This study locks ONE cohort
  (`vcp.tightness_range_factor=1.005`). Generalization to the other 4 binding
  variables (`vcp.tightness_days_required`, `vcp.adr_min_pct`,
  `vcp.proximity_max_pct`, `vcp.orderliness_max_bar_ratio`) requires
  additional cohort smoke runs.
